Dayonikin

Who is Dayon / Dayonikin?

Dayonikin (also known as Dayon) — born Dayon Quadar Keller — is a young, genre-blending Canadian artist and producer from Kitchener, Ontario.

What is HXS?

The HXS Community (often associated with the handles @dayonikin, @hxs.sj, or @klemmer02) serves as a collective — a central hub for independent music releases, lifestyle content, and creative projects.

MIND OVER MATTER (2025)

MIND OVER MATTER is the full-length debut album by Dayon, self-released on January 17, 2025 across all streaming platforms. Spanning 33 tracks and running 1 hour and 23 minutes, the project blends experimental rap, emo rap, trap, boom bap, and R&B into a cohesive, emotionally raw body of work.

The concept of the album revolves around perseverance and mental strength. Throughout the tracklist, Dayon confronts stress, overthinking, anger, depression, and deeply personal struggles. Much of the album was created during one of the most difficult periods of his life: facing a one-year jail sentence for assault, dealing with the loss of all his previous Dayonikin accounts, and navigating turbulent relationship challenges.

Under his producer alias Dayonikin, he self-produced key tracks including “Can’t Be Your Friend,” “One Way,” “I Know,” “Burdens,” “Laptop,” and “Amen.” Additional production contributions come from DWNLD, Rollie, cgkbeatz, and others, alongside notable collaborations with Lil Keel and long-time collaborator SJ (HXS SJ).

The release marks a major identity shift — transitioning from Dayonikin to Dayon as his official performing name — symbolizing a restart after reclaiming his artistic path.

A LIVING NIGHTMARE (2025)

A LIVING NIGHTMARE is a collaborative full-length album by Dayon and producer AVI Christian, released on February 1, 2025 through HXS Music Official. The 12-track project runs approximately 33 minutes and blends hip-hop and rap with atmospheric, emotionally charged production.

The album explores themes of fear, regret, self-doubt, and mental entrapment. AVI Christian’s cinematic and haunting production creates a dream-like yet unsettling environment, allowing Dayon’s vulnerability to sit at the forefront.

Though concise, the project delivers a powerful narrative arc and represents a continued evolution toward cohesive, conceptual storytelling.
